About

I am an Engineer II Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution (WHOI). My work spans dynamic mooring analysis, computational fluid dynamics, and autonomous surface vehicle operations in support of sustained ocean observation.

My M.S. research at University of Massachusetts focused on vortex-induced vibration (VIV) suppression in marine stretch hose moorings using helical grooves, demonstrating up to 90% VIV reduction and ~30% drag reduction through large-eddy simulation (LES) CFD.

I am motivated by the intersection of precision engineering and ocean science.

Projects

Deep-water mooring · ~6,000 m · North Pacific · ONR-funded · Joint WHOI–Scripps

SAFARI Mooring System

Dynamic mooring analysis for a deep-water mooring at ~6,000 m in the North Pacific. Assessed Wirewalker downweight configurations, developed JONSWAP sea state load cases, and identified critical failure risks in conventional downweight setups at full ocean depth.

Shallow-water mooring · 32 m · Nantucket Sound · DOE-funded

WFIP-3 LiDAR Buoy Mooring

Engineered a shallow-water mooring for an offshore wind LiDAR buoy in Nantucket Sound. Balanced simulation recommendations against site footprint constraints, producing a functional mooring with documented hardware stress margins.

Mooring analysis · ~2,300 m · Juan de Fuca Ridge · In progress

Endurance Energy Mooring

Ongoing ProteusDS dynamic mooring analysis for a MOBS single-leg surface expression mooring near the Juan de Fuca Ridge. Developing current velocity profiles from OOI RCA ADCP data, including surface extrapolation and formal load case documentation.

CFD simulation · ANSYS Fluent · JTECH

APEX Float Vortex Shedding Investigation

CFD investigation into Strouhal-frequency vortex shedding driving ~0.3 Hz accelerometer oscillations in APEX profiling floats. Included geometry reconstruction in Inventor, poly-hexcore meshing (~1.54 M cells), steady RANS (SST k-ω), and transient URANS analysis.

Subsurface mooring · 40–80 m · OMI Ghana

Ghana Subsurface Mooring Analysis

Engineering analysis for subsurface moorings at three depths (40/60/80 m). Produced knockdown analysis, current profiles, float buoyancy assessments, and pass/fail documentation. Developed a field installation guide for UT30 met tower concrete foundations.

M.S. thesis · UMass Dartmouth · LES CFD

VIV Suppression in Marine Stretch Hose Moorings

Graduate research using large-eddy simulation to evaluate helical groove geometry for VIV suppression in marine stretch hose moorings. Demonstrated up to 90% VIV and lift force reduction alongside ~30% drag reduction.
