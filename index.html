About

I am a Mechanical Engineer at the Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution (WHOI). My work focuses on the design, simulation, analysis, and fabrication of ocean engineering systems that support marine research, ocean observation, and field operations. My areas of expertise include hydrodynamics, computational fluid dynamics (CFD), hydro-mechanical systems, mooring technologies, AUV dynamics, and the design of reliable oceanographic systems for deployment in challenging marine environments.

I hold an M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from UMass Dartmouth, where my thesis focused on VIV suppression in marine stretch hose moorings using Large Eddy Simulation (LES), motivated by passive acoustic monitoring for North Atlantic Right Whales.

Projects

Selected engineering analysis, field support, and research contributions.

SAFARI Mooring

Deep-water (~6,000 m) mooring design for a joint WHOI–Scripps ONR-funded campaign in the North Pacific. ProteusDS dynamic analysis with downweight and compliance configurations.

WFIP-3 Offshore Wind

Mooring engineering support for the DOE-funded Wind Forecast Improvement Project in Nantucket Sound, including mooring design and field operations.

microALTO Float CFD

LES and RANS CFD investigation of vortex shedding from the microALTO profiling float (ANSYS Fluent). Supporting Le Boyer et al. 2025, JTECH.

Endurance Energy Mooring

Deepwater mooring analysis for the OOI Regional Cabled Array at Juan de Fuca Ridge (~2,300 m). Three load cases, fatigue analysis via Grosenbaugh (1995), LaTeX report.

VIV Suppression (Thesis)

LES-based CFD study of helical groove VIV suppression in marine stretch hose moorings, motivated by passive acoustic monitoring for North Atlantic Right Whales.

OMI Ghana Mooring

Subsurface mooring analysis (40/60/80 m cases) with ProteusDS for the Guinea Current, including corrected knockdown thresholds and revised current profiles.
