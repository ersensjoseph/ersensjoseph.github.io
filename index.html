About

I am a Mechanical Engineer working on dynamic mooring analysis, computational fluid dynamics, and autonomous surface vehicle operations in support of sustained ocean observation. I am motivated by the intersection of precision engineering and ocean science.

Research

  • Deep-water and shallow-water mooring system design and analysis
  • Computational fluid dynamics for marine structures
  • Vortex-induced vibration suppression
  • Autonomous surface and underwater vehicles
  • Ocean observing systems and engineering support

Current Roles

  • Engineer II, Woods Hole Oceanographic Institution
  • Member, MTS Buoy Technology

Projects

Deep-water mooring · ~6,000 m · North Pacific · ONR-funded · Joint WHOI–Scripps

SAFARI Mooring System

Dynamic mooring design and analysis for a deep-water mooring at full ocean depth. Assessed Wirewalker downweight configurations, and identified critical failure risks.

Mooring analysis · ~2,300 m · Juan de Fuca Ridge · In progress

Endurance Energy Mooring

Ongoing ProteusDS dynamic mooring analysis for a MOBS single-leg surface expression mooring, including current profile development from OOI RCA ADCP data and formal load case documentation.

Shallow-water mooring · 32 m · Nantucket Sound · DOE-funded

WFIP-3 LiDAR Buoy Mooring

Engineered a shallow-water mooring for an offshore wind LiDAR buoy, balancing simulation recommendations against site footprint constraints while documenting hardware stress margins.

CFD simulation · ANSYS Fluent · APEX float study

APEX Float Vortex Shedding Investigation

CFD investigation into Strouhal-frequency vortex shedding linked to accelerometer oscillations in APEX profiling floats, including geometry reconstruction, meshing, steady RANS, and transient URANS analysis.

Subsurface mooring · 40–80 m · OMI Ghana

Ghana Subsurface Mooring Analysis

Produced knockdown analysis, current profiles, float buoyancy assessments, pass/fail documentation, and a field installation guide for UT30 met tower concrete foundations.

M.S. thesis · UMass Dartmouth · LES CFD

VIV Suppression in Marine Stretch Hose Moorings

Graduate research evaluating helical groove geometry for VIV suppression in marine stretch hose moorings, demonstrating major reductions in VIV, lift forces, and drag.
